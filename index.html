The Event

One in ten Canadians faces some degree of hearing loss. Poor media representation of accessibility is a reality.

Wendy Tabor will discuss challenges to Deaf Canadians, including unemployment, by showcasing advocacy and presenting legal cases, such as Marlee Matlin's lawsuit against Netflix. This talk will shed light on successful legal actions and the importance of creating inclusive workplaces, drawing on Statistics Canada's findings on inclusive workplaces.

Wendy is a dedicated and Certified Registered Graphic Designer with expertise in navigating complex regulatory frameworks without compromising innovative design solutions. As a Hard-Of-Hearing individual, she brings a unique perspective to her work, championing inclusivity in every design endeavour.
