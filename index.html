The Event

Data plays a crucial role in storytelling, decision-making, and communication. But how do we ensure that data visualizations are accessible to everyone, including people with disabilities?

Join us on March 5th for a session exploring the intersection of inclusive design principles and data visualization. This talk will provide actionable insights into creating clear, effective, and accessible visual representations of data that go beyond aesthetics and prioritize usability for all audiences.

Our speaker, Sindhu Gadepalli, is a Product Designer with expertise in product discovery and research. With a focus on diverse and inclusive user experiences, she has helped shape products that are both visually compelling and functionally accessible. Currently pursuing a Master’s in Design Management at Savannah College of Art and Design, she is also consulting as a Founding Designer with a startup.

What We’ll Cover:

  • How inclusive design principles apply to data visualization
  • Common barriers in data accessibility and how to address them
  • Strategies for creating visualizations that work for a diverse audience
  • Real-world examples of effective and inclusive data communication

This session is ideal for designers, product managers, analysts, developers, and anyone interested in making data-driven content more inclusive.

Important Notes:

  • Registration is required to attend. Space is limited, so sign up early.
  • This is an in-person event at the League Office.

We look forward to seeing you there!
